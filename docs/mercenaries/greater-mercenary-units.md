# Greater Mercenary Companies

> *“The Houses rule worlds. The Greater Companies decide who survives between them.”*  
> — Guild strategic assessment

Greater Mercenary Companies are the largest and most powerful mercenary organizations operating throughout the Core and frontier regions.

Unlike ordinary mercenary units, Greater Companies function as massive institutional military organizations possessing:
- fleets
- logistics networks
- recruitment infrastructure
- repair facilities
- long-term charters
- standing military formations

Some rival smaller planetary governments in military capability and economic influence.

Although technically private military organizations, Greater Companies often possess enough political leverage to influence:
- frontier stability
- trade security
- regional conflicts
- House relations
- Guild policy

Throughout civilized space, they are viewed simultaneously as:
- military contractors
- strategic assets
- political actors
- stabilizing forces
- potential threats

## Origins

The rise of the Greater Companies began during the centuries following the Great Restoration.

As mercenary warfare became increasingly normalized throughout the Core, certain successful companies accumulated enough:
- wealth
- salvage
- territory
- personnel
- logistical capability

to expand far beyond the scale of traditional contract forces.

Over generations, some evolved from relatively small mercenary outfits into massive military institutions operating across multiple systems.

Unlike temporary frontier companies or independent Free Jack crews, Greater Companies developed permanent organizational structures capable of surviving beyond the lifespan of individual commanders.

This transformed them from simple mercenary units into enduring military powers.

## The Three Greater Companies

Although countless mercenary companies operate throughout civilized space, only three are universally recognized as the true Greater Mercenary Companies of the modern era.

These organizations possess such immense military and logistical capability that many political analysts consider them quasi-state powers operating outside direct House authority.

## The Nomad Company

|  |  |
|---|---|
| :material-account-star: **Supreme Commander** | Field Lord |
| :material-shield-sword: **Combat Doctrine** | Relentless Assault Warfare |
| :material-rocket-launch: **Primary Asset** | Mobile Dreadnought Fortress |
| :material-handshake: **Frequent Employer** | Starcrest Protectorate |

The Nomad Company is one of the largest and most feared mercenary organizations in the Core.

The company is renowned for deploying overwhelming battlefield force through:
- massive mech formations
- sustained assaults
- aggressive maneuver warfare
- attritional campaigns

The Nomads possess a reputation for capturing enemy equipment intact whenever possible, allowing them to expand and maintain their forces through battlefield salvage as much as formal production.

Unlike many large mercenary commands, the Nomad Company maintains no permanent planetary capital.

Instead, its operational headquarters is housed within a heavily modified dreadnought battle cruiser capable of sustaining long-term deep-space operations.

This mobile fortress serves simultaneously as:
- command center
- fleet anchor
- training base
- logistics hub
- symbolic homeworld

The company officially accepts contracts from all Great Houses, though it maintains especially close operational ties with the Starcrest Protectorate.

Among mercenaries, Nomad forces are often viewed as relentless, disciplined, and extraordinarily difficult to destroy completely.

## The Warforged Convoy

|  |  |
|---|---|
| :material-account-star: **Supreme Commander** | War Marshal |
| :material-hammer-wrench: **Combat Doctrine** | Adaptive Mechanized Warfare |
| :material-robot-industrial: **Specialization** | Heavy Mech Modification |
| :material-handshake: **Frequent Employer** | Confederate Vanguard Union |

The Warforged Convoy is famous throughout the Core for its heavily modified and extensively customized mech forces.

The company’s mechs are maintained and redesigned by highly skilled mechwrights capable of radically altering battlefield configurations between deployments.

Warforged pilots are often trained as both:
- mech operators
- field engineers
- combat technicians

This allows Warforged units to rapidly adapt equipment and weapon systems to changing battlefield conditions.

Enemy commanders frequently struggle to identify Warforged units accurately, as the company’s modified mechs rarely conform to standard military specifications.

The Convoy’s flexible combat doctrine and engineering culture have earned it a formidable reputation across frontier warzones.

The Confederate Vanguard Union is known to employ the Warforged Convoy extensively for:
- offensive campaigns
- mechanized breakthroughs
- industrial warfare
- heavy assault operations

Within mercenary circles, the Warforged are respected for their technical expertise and feared for the unpredictability of their steel.

## Iron Phoenix Regulars

|  |  |
|---|---|
| :material-account-star: **Supreme Commander** | Red Commander |
| :material-chess-queen: **Combat Doctrine** | Strategic Precision Operations |
| :material-sword-cross: **Specialization** | Elite Raids and Tactical Operations |
| :material-handshake: **Frequent Employers** | Helios Sovereignty and Omnisphere Imperium |

The Iron Phoenix Regulars are widely regarded as among the most disciplined and strategically sophisticated mercenary forces in civilized space.

Unlike the overwhelming assaults favored by the Nomad Company or the adaptive mechanized warfare of the Warforged Convoy, the Regulars specialize in:
- precise operational planning
- elite strike missions
- strategic raids
- extraction operations
- targeted battlefield disruption

The Regulars maintain an unusually strict internal code of conduct by mercenary standards.

This professionalism has earned them considerable respect throughout the Core, including among many rival commanders.

Their elite strike teams are particularly famous for conducting high-profile operations capable of influencing broader geopolitical events.

The Helios Sovereignty and Omnisphere Imperium frequently employ the Iron Phoenix Regulars for missions requiring:
- discretion
- coordination
- strategic discipline
- political sensitivity

Among mercenary circles, the Regulars are often viewed as the closest thing the profession possesses to a true elite officer corps.

## Relationship with the Great Houses

All five Great Houses employ Greater Mercenary Companies to varying degrees.

These organizations are especially valuable for:
- frontier stabilization
- anti-piracy campaigns
- strategic deniability
- expeditionary warfare
- border conflicts
- politically sensitive operations

Although technically independent, many Greater Companies maintain long-standing historical ties with specific Houses.

Some are widely believed to function as unofficial House auxiliaries despite maintaining nominal neutrality.

The Houses tolerate the existence of Greater Companies because they provide military flexibility without requiring direct inter-house warfare.

At the same time, many political leaders remain deeply uncomfortable with the sheer military power these organizations possess.

## Relationship with the Mercenary Guild

The Mercenary Guild plays a critical role in regulating Greater Companies.

Because of their scale and strategic importance, Greater Companies are deeply integrated into:
- Guild arbitration systems
- charter regulation
- contract enforcement
- salvage oversight
- reputation systems

Many Greater Companies possess substantial political influence within Guild institutions themselves.

Smaller mercenary outfits occasionally accuse the Guild of favoring Greater Companies through:
- preferential contracts
- arbitration bias
- salvage rulings
- enforcement discretion

Guild officials strongly deny such accusations.

## Lesser and Minor Companies

Below the Greater Companies exist countless:
- Lesser Mercenary Companies
- Minor Mercenary Companies
- Free Jack crews

The overwhelming majority of mercenary organizations throughout the Core fall into these smaller classifications.

Many survive by accepting:
- subcontract work
- escort charters
- salvage operations
- local defense contracts
- anti-piracy assignments

from Greater Companies.

This creates a layered mercenary economy in which major military operations frequently involve networks of subordinate or affiliated companies operating beneath larger banners.

## Mercenary Culture

Greater Companies possess powerful internal cultures and identities.

Veteran mercenaries often identify more strongly with their company banner than with any Great House.

Large companies develop:
- traditions
- heraldry
- rivalries
- operational doctrines
- command philosophies
- internal myths

Among mercenaries, reputation matters enormously.

A respected company banner may attract:
- elite pilots
- favorable contracts
- long charters
- House sponsorship
- premium Guild access

while a burned banner may struggle to survive.

## Public Reputation

Throughout civilized space, the Greater Mercenary Companies are viewed with a mixture of:
- admiration
- fear
- dependence
- resentment
- respect

To frontier worlds, they may represent the only reliable military protection available.

To the Great Houses, they are useful but potentially dangerous strategic actors.

To independent mercenaries, they are often viewed either as:
- aspirational legends
- bloated institutions
- contract machines
- or the ultimate destination of a successful mercenary career

As one old Landing saying goes:

> *“Minor crews chase contracts. Greater Companies chase sectors.”*

## Modern Outlook

As instability spreads throughout the Core and frontier alike, the influence of the Greater Mercenary Companies continues to grow.

Some StarCom analysts privately warn that several Greater Companies now possess enough military and logistical capability to destabilize entire sectors should they ever fully abandon Guild oversight.

Others argue the Greater Companies have become indispensable to maintaining modern civilization itself.

For now, the balance holds.

The Great Houses still rule the Core.

But increasingly, the wars between them are fought not beneath House banners —

but beneath the steel banners of the Greater Companies.
