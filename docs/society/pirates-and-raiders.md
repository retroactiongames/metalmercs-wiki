# Pirates and Raiders

Piracy remains one of the most persistent threats throughout the Fringe Worlds and Frontier regions. While large-scale naval warfare between the Great Houses is relatively rare, smaller conflicts involving pirate fleets, raider bands, smugglers, and criminal syndicates occur constantly beyond the heavily patrolled Core systems.

Pirate groups vary enormously in size and organization. Some consist of little more than a handful of lightly armed ships targeting isolated cargo convoys and frontier settlements. Others operate as highly organized criminal enterprises controlling hidden bases, smuggling networks, salvage operations, and black-market trade routes across multiple systems.

Most pirate activity occurs within the Fringe, where the authority of the Great Houses weakens and civilian shipping routes become more difficult to protect. Merchant convoys traveling through frontier systems frequently hire mercenary escorts, while many independent settlements maintain extensive local defenses against raids.

Pirate bases are commonly hidden on remote moons, asteroid belts, abandoned mining colonies, or forgotten Imperial facilities. Many such outposts are intentionally located far from major jump routes to avoid detection by House patrols or StarCom monitoring stations. Some larger pirate enclaves function as full criminal settlements complete with repair docks, marketplaces, refineries, gambling halls, and black-market trade hubs.

Raiders differ somewhat from traditional pirates in both organization and objectives. Whereas pirates generally focus on smuggling, theft, and profit, raider groups are often more militarized and destructive. Raider attacks may target civilian settlements directly, seizing supplies, fuel, industrial equipment, or prisoners before withdrawing into poorly charted frontier regions.

Numerous criminal organizations operate within pirate-controlled territories. Smugglers traffic in illegal weapons, military technology, narcotics, forged identification systems, and stolen industrial goods. Salvage gangs frequently strip abandoned Imperial ruins or battlefields for valuable components and weapons systems.

Relations between pirate groups and the Great Houses are often more complicated than officially acknowledged. Though all major governments publicly condemn piracy, House intelligence agencies have at times been accused of covertly supporting certain pirate organizations for political or strategic purposes. Pirates may also serve as intermediaries for smuggling operations, deniable attacks, or black-market trade networks connected to larger political interests.

Mercenary companies frequently conduct anti-piracy operations throughout the Fringe. Escort missions, bounty contracts, base assaults, and convoy defense operations represent a major source of employment for smaller mercenary units. Successful pirate hunters can become highly respected figures within frontier regions, while notorious pirate commanders may achieve infamy throughout the Core.

For ordinary civilians living near the Fringe, piracy is often viewed as an unavoidable reality of frontier life. Many settlements maintain emergency bunkers, planetary militia forces, and warning systems specifically designed to respond to pirate attacks. Convoy delays, supply shortages, and security alerts are common occurrences on remote worlds.

Within Core media and popular culture, pirates occupy a contradictory role. Official propaganda portrays them as violent criminals threatening civilized space, while entertainment programs often romanticize pirate life as rebellious and adventurous. Frontier folklore similarly contains countless stories of hidden treasure fleets, rogue admirals, lost pirate kingdoms, and legendary raider captains operating beyond the reach of House authority.

Despite ongoing military campaigns and security operations, piracy persists because of the immense scale of human space itself. The Great Houses simply cannot patrol every trade route, moon, asteroid field, and isolated colony scattered across the Fringe and Frontier.
