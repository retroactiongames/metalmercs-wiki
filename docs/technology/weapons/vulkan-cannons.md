## Overview

Vulkan Cannons are heavy ballistic weapons designed specifically for engaging armored targets at range.

Unlike Autoguns, which rely on sustained fire, Vulkan Cannons launch large-caliber shells capable of inflicting devastating damage with a single hit. Their combination of firepower, reliability, and relatively low heat generation has made them one of the most widely used anti-mech weapons in the Core.

Though often overshadowed by more advanced technologies, few commanders underestimate the destructive potential of a well-placed Vulkan round.

---

## Battlefield Role

Vulkan Cannons serve as the primary long-range ballistic weapon for many military forces.

The weapon's greatest strength is its ability to deliver substantial damage while generating comparatively little heat. This allows Vulkan-equipped mechs to maintain consistent fire over extended engagements without suffering the thermal limitations common to many energy weapons.

Their high-impact shells are particularly effective against heavily armored targets. Even when a round fails to penetrate armor completely, the kinetic force of the impact can degrade protective plating, strip armor, and create opportunities for follow-up attacks.

The primary drawback of the Vulkan Cannon is ammunition dependency. Unlike energy weapons, Vulkan-equipped units must maintain a steady supply of shells during prolonged operations. Ammunition shortages can quickly reduce even the most powerful cannon-equipped mech to a liability.

Vulkan Cannons are especially common within the Confederate Vanguard Union, the Starcrest Protectorate, and the Omnisphere Imperium. Their reputation for reliability and straightforward battlefield effectiveness aligns closely with the military doctrines of all three powers.

## History

Vulkan Cannons trace their lineage back thousands of years and are among the oldest mech-scale weapon systems still in widespread military service.

While individual designs have evolved considerably over the centuries, the underlying concept has remained remarkably consistent: launch a large projectile at high velocity and allow mass, momentum, and kinetic energy to do the rest.

The weapon's longevity stems from its simplicity. Unlike many advanced energy weapons, Vulkan Cannons require no exotic focusing arrays, specialized reactor technologies, or proprietary manufacturing techniques. They are straightforward, dependable machines capable of delivering tremendous firepower under almost any battlefield conditions.

Over time, many military powers experimented with more sophisticated ballistic systems. The most successful of these efforts produced the Magnetic Accelerator Cannon (MAC), a weapon that eventually replaced the Vulkan Cannon within the Helios Sovereignty. While more expensive and technologically demanding, MACs offered superior range, accuracy, and armor penetration, making them a natural fit for Sovereignty doctrine.

Elsewhere, however, Vulkan Cannons remained dominant. The Union, Protectorate, and Imperium continue to field large numbers of Vulkan-equipped mechs, valuing their reliability, ease of maintenance, and proven battlefield effectiveness.

Today, Vulkan Cannons remain one of the defining weapons of modern mech warfare. Though newer technologies continue to emerge, few weapons can match the combination of simplicity, affordability, and raw stopping power that has kept the Vulkan relevant for millennia.
