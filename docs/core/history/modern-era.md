# The Modern Era

The Modern Era refers to the period following the [Great Restoration](restoration.md) and the establishment of the political institutions that continue to govern the Core today.

It is an age of relative prosperity, expanding trade, and renewed civilization. For centuries, the [Core](../) has enjoyed a degree of stability unimaginable during the [Dark Age](dark-age.md). Interstellar commerce flourishes, major trade routes remain secure, and most citizens live their entire lives without experiencing major warfare.

Yet beneath this stability lies a growing sense of uncertainty.

The institutions that rebuilt civilization following the Fall remain powerful, but many observers question whether they remain strong enough to withstand the challenges of the modern age.

## The Restoration Order

The Modern Era began with the creation of the Restoration Order, a collection of institutions designed to prevent humanity from ever descending into another Dark Age.

These institutions include:

* The [Star Regency](../../factions/star-regent.md)
* The Stellar Conclave
* The [Great Houses](../../factions/)
* [StarCom](../../factions/starcom.md)
* The [Mercenary Guild](../../mercenaries/guild/)
* The restored interstellar trade network

Together, these organizations form the framework through which the Core governs itself.

Though individual Houses maintain considerable autonomy, most major disputes are resolved through diplomacy, economic competition, limited military actions, or the mediation of the Conclave rather than open warfare.

For centuries, this system has succeeded in maintaining peace.

## The Great Houses

The modern [Core](../) is dominated by five [Great Houses](../../factions/).

Unlike the countless smaller houses that existed during the Age of Empire, the Great Houses control enormous territories spanning hundreds of worlds and command military forces rivaling those of entire sectors from earlier eras.

Though officially united under the authority of the Stellar Conclave, each House pursues its own interests and ambitions.

The balance between these powers forms the foundation of modern Core politics.

Supporters view the Great Houses as guardians of civilization. Critics argue they are little more than rival kingdoms restrained only by mutual fear and economic necessity.

## The Long Peace

The centuries following the Restoration are often referred to as the Long Peace.

While conflicts have certainly occurred, no war has approached the scale or devastation of the Fall or the Dark Age. Trade expanded steadily. Population centers grew. Scientific knowledge gradually recovered. New colonies were established. Once-abandoned worlds were resettled.

Many citizens regard this period as the most prosperous era in recorded history since the [Empire](the-empire.md) itself.

Yet historians caution against such comparisons.

The modern [Core](../) remains vastly smaller than the [Empire](the-empire.md), possesses only fragments of its technological knowledge, and lacks many of the institutions that once maintained galactic unity. In many ways, the Core resembles a successful successor state built atop the ruins of a far greater civilization.

## Technology and Recovery

Much of modern technology traces its origins to discoveries, maintenance traditions, and recovered knowledge inherited from the [Age of Empire](the-empire.md).

While innovation certainly occurs, many technologies remain poorly understood. Engineers often know how to build, repair, and improve systems without fully understanding the scientific principles behind them.

Ancient facilities, forgotten data archives, and lost technologies remain among the most valuable discoveries in known space.

As a result, exploration and salvage continue to play important roles throughout the Core economy.

Entire fortunes have been built upon the recovery of forgotten knowledge.

## Mercenaries and Limited War

One of the defining features of the Modern Era is the widespread use of [mercenary](../../mercenaries/) forces.

The Great Houses frequently compete with one another, but rarely seek direct confrontation. Instead, disputes are often fought through proxy wars, frontier conflicts, private military contracts, and deniable operations.

[Mercenary companies](../../mercenaries/) fill this role.

Ranging from small independent commands to vast military organizations controlling entire fleets, mercenaries have become an accepted part of the political landscape. In some regions, they are viewed as neutral professionals. In others, they are little more than legalized warlords.

Their influence is so significant that many observers consider the [Mercenary Guild](../../mercenaries/guild/) to be one of the most important institutions in the Core.

## The Frontier

Despite centuries of recovery, much of human space remains unsettled.

Beyond the secure trade routes and prosperous Core worlds lie the [Frontier](../../places/the-frontier.md), the Fringe, the Badlands, and countless isolated systems.

Some are home to thriving colonies. Others are ruled by pirates, warlords, criminal syndicates, or independent powers. Still others contain forgotten relics of earlier ages.

These regions provide opportunity and danger in equal measure.

Many fortunes are made beyond the borders of civilized space. Many more are lost.

## Rising Tensions

Although the Modern Era is often viewed as an age of peace, few believe the current balance will last forever.

Competition between the Great Houses continues to intensify. Political rivalries grow sharper. Economic disputes become more frequent. Frontier conflicts have increased in both scale and frequency. New technologies continue to alter the military balance between competing powers.

At the same time, confidence in the institutions of the Restoration Order has begun to weaken.

Some believe the Stellar Conclave has become ineffective. Others argue that [StarCom](../../factions/starcom.md) exerts too much influence over interstellar affairs. Still others question whether the Great Houses remain committed to cooperation at all.

Many observers see troubling similarities between the present era and the final centuries of the Empire.

## A Turning Point

Whether these concerns are justified remains uncertain.

To some, the Core stands on the brink of a new golden age. Trade reaches farther than ever before. Exploration pushes into previously inaccessible regions. Scientific discoveries continue to reshape society.

To others, the warning signs are unmistakable.

The Core has grown wealthy. It has grown powerful. It has grown complacent.

The [Empire](the-empire.md) once stood in the same position.

What followed is remembered as the Fall.

Whether the Modern Era represents the beginning of humanity's next great age—or the final years before another collapse—remains one of the defining questions of the age.
