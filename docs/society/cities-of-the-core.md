# Cities of the Core

The cities of the Core are among the largest and most densely populated human settlements in known space. Built atop layers of older Imperial infrastructure accumulated across centuries, many Core cities stretch for hundreds of kilometers and contain populations numbering in the tens or even hundreds of millions.

Modern Core cities are typically composed of multiple historical layers. At their centers stand the active urban districts of the modern age: dense commercial sectors, government centers, industrial zones, residential arcologies, and transportation hubs maintained by the Great Houses and local planetary authorities. Beneath and beyond these active districts however lie older regions dating back to the Empire, many of which now stand abandoned or only partially inhabited.

Towering arcologies dominate the skylines of many wealthy Core worlds. These immense self-contained structures house residential sectors, commercial districts, industrial facilities, transit systems, and public services within single interconnected megastructures. Some arcologies contain populations equivalent to entire cities on their own.

Infrastructure within the Core is heavily dependent upon ancient Imperial systems still maintained centuries after their construction. Automated transit networks, subterranean maglev systems, orbital elevators, atmospheric processors, and industrial fabrication complexes remain essential to daily life. Though modern engineers can maintain and repair much of this infrastructure, much of its original design and construction methods are only partially understood.

Social inequality is common within many cities of the Core. Wealthy districts are often clean, heavily secured, and technologically sophisticated, populated by political elites, corporate executives, military officers, and influential merchant families. Lower industrial sectors and labor districts are typically more crowded and economically unstable, particularly on manufacturing and refinery worlds.

Beneath many major cities lie vast underlevels and abandoned sectors collectively referred to by numerous local names such as the Undercity, Lower Zones, Dead Levels, or Old Districts. These areas often contain forgotten transit tunnels, collapsed industrial facilities, abandoned residential sectors, and sealed military infrastructure left behind from earlier periods of Imperial expansion.

Some abandoned districts have become home to criminal organizations, smugglers, salvage crews, undocumented populations, and black-market industries operating beyond effective government oversight. Local authorities frequently tolerate such activity so long as it remains contained within lower sectors and does not threaten the stability of the upper city.

Security within major Core cities is extensive. Surveillance systems, automated checkpoints, local security forces, and House military patrols are common in politically important urban centers. Despite this, organized crime, political corruption, sabotage, and espionage remain widespread beneath the surface of civilized society.

Daily life within the cities of the Core is shaped by both modern civilization and the lingering presence of the Empire. Citizens may commute through transit tunnels older than their governments, live within structures constructed centuries earlier, or work alongside machinery whose original manufacturers no longer exist.

For many citizens the cities of the Core represent the height of human civilization: safe, prosperous, technologically advanced, and culturally vibrant. At the same time they also serve as reminders of humanity’s gradual decline. No modern government could fully recreate the immense urban systems inherited from the Empire, and much of the ancient cityscape now exists as silent ruins surrounding the surviving centers of civilization.
