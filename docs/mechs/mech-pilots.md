# Mech Pilots

Mech pilots occupy a unique place within Core society. Though vastly outnumbered by conventional soldiers, pilots command the most powerful battlefield assets available to modern militaries and are widely regarded as elite personnel.

Most Great Houses maintain dedicated military academies that train pilots over periods ranging from one to two years. These programs combine simulator instruction, live-fire exercises, battlefield doctrine, logistics, leadership, and conventional military education. Advanced simulators and training mechs allow cadets to participate in large-scale war games before entering active service.

While all major powers produce capable pilots, training philosophies vary. The Confederate Vanguard Union emphasizes discipline, logistics, and combined-arms doctrine. The Omnisphere Imperium prioritizes individual excellence and martial achievement. The Starcrest Protectorate is known for harsh training regimes designed to produce resilient pilots capable of enduring extreme battlefield conditions. The Helios Sovereignty maintains prestigious academies, though critics occasionally point to the influence of wealth and family connections. Orion Corporate programs are technologically sophisticated but lack the centuries of combat experience available to some rival powers.

Outside the Great Houses, standards vary dramatically. Mercenary companies may spend months training recruits, while bandit groups often place barely qualified pilots into combat-ready machines.

The public image of mech pilots is overwhelmingly positive. Popular media frequently portrays them as heroes, adventurers, or modern-day knights. Many pilots adopt callsigns, cultivate rivalries, and participate in a highly competitive culture reminiscent of fighter aces from earlier eras.

Despite the dangers of combat, piloting is considered a relatively survivable profession compared to infantry service or aerospace combat. Modern ejection systems, battlefield recovery operations, and longstanding customs surrounding escape pods have contributed to career lengths that often span decades.
