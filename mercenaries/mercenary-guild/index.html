    Mercenary Guild

    “Where words bind war.”
    — Guild motto

    Overview
    Organization Type Mercenary Regulatory Authority
    Headquarters Drake’s Landing
    Primary Function Contract Arbitration and Mercenary Oversight
    Political Status Neutral Institution
    Primary Influence Licensing, Contracts, Reputation Systems
    Guild Motto “Where Words Bind War.”

    The Mercenary Guild is the primary regulatory institution overseeing mercenary activity throughout the Core and frontier regions.

    Officially neutral and recognized by both the Star Regency and the Great Houses, the Guild exists to regulate, formalize, and contain the enormous system of private warfare that defines much of the modern era.

    The Guild oversees: - mercenary licensing - contract arbitration - company registration - payment enforcement - salvage claims - reputation tracking - blacklists - operational disputes

    Most legal mercenary work throughout civilized space passes through the Guild Open Board, where registered companies review available contracts, negotiate terms, and secure Guild-backed payment guarantees.

    Although the Guild does not possess the military power of the Great Houses, its economic and legal authority grants it enormous influence throughout the Core.

    In practice, the Guild functions as one of the primary institutions preventing mercenary warfare from descending into uncontrolled chaos.

    Origins

    The Mercenary Guild emerged during the centuries following the Great Restoration.

    As civilization stabilized under the Star Regency, conflicts between the Great Houses increasingly shifted toward: - proxy conflicts - frontier disputes - covert operations - deniable military action

    Mercenary companies expanded rapidly during this period.

    Initially, the absence of regulation created widespread instability involving: - contract fraud - piracy - unauthorized escalation - civilian massacres - salvage disputes - inter-company warfare - abandoned dead claims

    Several early mercenary conflicts reportedly came close to destabilizing entire frontier sectors.

    Recognizing that mercenary warfare could not realistically be eliminated, the Regency and Great Houses instead supported the creation of a neutral institution capable of regulating it.

    Thus the Mercenary Guild was formed.

    Philosophy

    The Guild’s guiding principle is summarized in its motto:

    “Where Words Bind War.”

    Within Guild doctrine, contracts are viewed not merely as financial agreements, but as the foundation preventing civilization from collapsing into unrestricted violence.

    Guild officials frequently argue that warfare itself is inevitable.

    What matters is whether it remains: - regulated - enforceable - limited - survivable

    To the Guild, signed terms are civilization’s final restraint upon chaos.

    Drake’s Landing

    The Guild maintains its central headquarters on Drake’s Landing, the mercenary capital of civilized space.

    The world serves as: - the largest Open Board hub in the Core - a major mech repair center - a contract exchange nexus - a recruitment hub - a salvage market - neutral negotiation territory

    Thousands of pilots, crews, dock workers, mechanics, and independent companies pass through Drake’s Landing every cycle.

    Guild influence is strongest there.

    Many frontier pilots joke that a company is not truly legitimate until it has survived at least one contract season on Landing.

    Licensing and Registration

    Most recognized mercenary companies operate under Guild registration.

    Registered companies receive: - access to the Open Board - Guild arbitration protection - salvage recognition - payment guarantees - verified steel certification - recognized operational status

    Operating outside Guild authority is technically possible, particularly in deep frontier space, but unlicensed companies are frequently viewed as pirates or raiders.

    The Guild maintains extensive operational records tracking: - contract fulfillment - battlefield conduct - civilian casualty reports - salvage disputes - disciplinary actions - company reputation

    These records heavily influence future contract opportunities.

    Reputation and Blacklisting

    Within mercenary culture, reputation is often more valuable than military strength.

    Reliable companies with clean Guild records frequently receive: - higher-paying contracts - priority claims - House-backed work - favorable salvage rights - long-term charter agreements

    Meanwhile, companies accused of: - contract violations - piracy - civilian atrocities - employer betrayal - unauthorized escalation

    may receive formal Guild sanctions or burn notices.

    Severe violations can result in blacklisting.

    A blacklisted company often becomes effectively unemployable within civilized space.

    Many eventually drift into raiding, piracy, or grey salvage operations beyond Guild jurisdiction.

    Contract Arbitration

    The Guild serves as the primary arbitration authority for mercenary disputes throughout the Core.

    Guild arbitration courts oversee: - payment disputes - contract interpretation - salvage ownership - operational disagreements - breach accusations

    When disputes occur, Guild arbitrators may issue: - arbitration locks - payment freezes - temporary suspensions - operational sanctions

    Most major powers comply with Guild rulings because maintaining confidence in the mercenary system is considered strategically essential.

    Without arbitration, much of frontier commerce and security would rapidly collapse.

    Enforcement

    Contrary to popular belief, the Guild is not a major military power.

    It maintains: - station defense forces - limited patrol fleets - enforcement detachments - investigative divisions

    primarily for protecting Guild infrastructure and personnel.

    The Guild’s true authority derives from: - economic legitimacy - legal recognition - reputation systems - political necessity

    The Great Houses tolerate and support the Guild because it prevents mercenary conflicts from escalating into direct inter-house warfare.

    Relationship with the Great Houses

    All five Great Houses make extensive use of mercenary companies.

    Officially, mercenary contracts are used for: - frontier defense - anti-piracy operations - convoy escort - infrastructure security

    Unofficially, many companies participate in: - proxy wars - covert operations - territorial disputes - political sabotage - quiet work

    House sponsorship of mercenary companies is common, though often concealed behind intermediaries and shell contracts.

    The Guild generally avoids direct involvement in House rivalries whenever possible.

    Maintaining neutrality is considered essential to preserving institutional legitimacy.

    Relationship with the Regency

    The Star Regency officially recognizes the Mercenary Guild as a neutral civil institution.

    Many political observers believe the Regency quietly depends upon the Guild to contain conflicts that might otherwise escalate into open warfare between the Great Houses.

    Although House Caledon occasionally pressures the Guild during periods of severe instability, the Guild has historically resisted becoming an outright arm of the Regency.

    This balance between cooperation and independence remains politically delicate.

    Guild Culture

    Guild culture tends to be highly procedural, pragmatic, and politically cautious.

    Guild officials are generally less concerned with ideology than with: - enforceable agreements - operational stability - predictable conduct - conflict containment

    Among mercenaries, the Guild possesses a mixed reputation.

    Some pilots view it as: - civilization’s necessary referee - the protector of legitimate companies - the only thing keeping the Open Board functional

    Others view it as: - bureaucratic - politically compromised - overly favorable toward large companies and House-backed outfits

    Despite criticism, few mercenary companies willingly operate outside Guild systems for long.

    Public Reputation

    Throughout the Core, the Mercenary Guild is viewed as: - neutral - bureaucratic - influential - necessary - pragmatic

    To frontier populations, Guild certification often represents the difference between legitimate protection and outright piracy.

    To the Great Houses, the Guild serves as both a useful political instrument and a stabilizing institution.

    To mercenaries themselves, the Guild is simply part of life.

    As one old Landing saying goes:

    “No steel rides long without a Guild seal.”

    Modern Outlook

    As tensions between the Great Houses continue to rise, the Mercenary Guild faces growing pressure from every direction.

    Frontier instability, pirate activity, expanding proxy conflicts, and increasingly aggressive House politics have strained the systems established after the Great Restoration.

    Many within the Guild fear that if its authority weakens too severely, the Core may once again descend into unrestricted warfare.

    For this reason, the Guild continues defending the principle upon which it was founded:

    Where Words Bind War.
