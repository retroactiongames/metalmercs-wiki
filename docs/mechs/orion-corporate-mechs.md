# Orion Mechs

Orion mech forces combine standard Core platforms with technologically advanced machines emphasizing sensors, precision, experimental systems, and battlefield efficiency.

This roster includes both the common Core designs available to all major factions and the signature designs most closely associated with the Orion Corporate.

## Common Core Designs

### Hornet `HN-120`

One of the oldest light designs still in circulation, the Hornet was cheap to manufacture and simple to maintain. Armed with twin light lasers, it was never meant to duel, but rather to scout, harass, and sting at vulnerable points before fleeing. Its proliferation across the Core is owed to its reliability and low cost—where there is conflict, Hornets are never far behind.

### Lynx `LNX-40E`

The Lynx became popular during the border wars for its balance of mobility and firepower. With paired medium lasers and a small rocket rack, it could serve as both flanker and support unit. Though newer mechs have surpassed it, the Lynx endures thanks to its rugged frame and versatility—qualities that made it a staple in every House’s arsenal.

### Outrider `OR-640`

Built for patrol duty and frontier policing, the Outrider combined endurance with straightforward weaponry. Its heavy and medium lasers gave it enough punch to deter raiders without requiring complex supply lines. While unimpressive by modern standards, the Outrider’s long service life and adaptability have ensured its presence in garrisons across the Core.

### Yeoman `YM-10F`

The Yeoman was designed as a dependable multi-role platform for Houses lacking specialized production. Its mixed loadout—autoguns, lasers, and scorchers—made it a jack-of-all-trades capable of handling infantry, armor, or other mechs in a pinch. Though rarely a commander’s first choice, the Yeoman’s steady performance earned it a reputation as the soldier’s friend.

### Lineman `LM-30C`

Heavy, slow, and built to endure punishment, the Lineman was conceived as a frontline brawler to hold the line while others maneuvered. Its vulkan cannon and paired autoguns provided steady suppressive fire. Though outclassed by newer heavies, it remains common wherever Houses need durable firepower to anchor defenses.

### Lancer `LNC-220`

The Lancer was among the first medium-heavies to emphasize precision energy fire over massed rockets. Armed with a heavy laser and layered support beams, it excelled at punching through weakened armor. Once hailed as a duelist’s machine, it is now more often used as a training platform or second-line support, though many veteran pilots still prize its accuracy.

### Longstrike `LG-210`

An aging missile platform, the Longstrike was originally deployed to provide sustained bombardment from behind the lines. With its paired racks and autogun backups, it could soften enemy positions before an assault. Though slow and vulnerable up close, its simplicity and ease of production have kept it in steady circulation across the Core.

### Champion `CH-70K`

Once built to serve in ceremonial tournaments as well as war, the Champion balanced missile fire with lasers and a vulkan. Though never as specialized as later duelists, it gained popularity as a reliable all-rounder. Many Houses still field Champions in honor guards or training battalions, where their long heritage and recognizable silhouette command respect.

### Bulldog `BD-770`

The Bulldog earned its name in countless sieges, battering defenses with high energy cannons and rocket salvos. Slow but stubborn, it excelled in drawn-out attrition battles. Even today, the Bulldog remains a favored choice for militias and second-line forces who need a hard-hitting mech that can take punishment and keep advancing.

### Marksman `MK-350`

The Marksman was designed as a fire-support chassis, mounting multiple vulkans for sustained barrages. Though criticized for its heat inefficiency and limited armor, it carved a niche as a battlefield “sweeper,” punishing light mechs and infantry that strayed too close. In modern times, it is often relegated to defensive roles, but its name still carries weight.

### Gladiator `GL-882`

A relic of the arena era, the Gladiator was built for spectacle as much as war. With rockets, vulkan, and lasers, it was capable of delivering flashy, punishing blows that made it a crowd favorite. Many Houses retired theirs to ceremonial duty, but in poorer regions of the Core, Gladiators still see combat, proving that even showpieces can draw blood.

### Kingsguard `KG-950`

The Kingsguard was once marketed as the ultimate “command mech,” a prestige unit to accompany noble lords into battle. Its missiles and mixed weaponry made it flexible, while its armor kept its pilot secure. Though time has dulled its reputation, the Kingsguard endures as a symbol of authority on the battlefield—still favored by militia leaders and House scions alike.


## Signature Orion Designs

### Photon `PHN-50T`

Developed by the Sentinel Program under the Orion Standards Commission, the Photon was the first widely fielded scout with projected shielding. Its medium prismatic array doubles as a sensor-liaison tool, letting it tag targets and survive brushes that would scrap other lights.

### Tachyon `TCY-2R`

An offshoot of the Photon II trials, the Tachyon prioritized velocity and precision fire for counter-raider screens. Twin light and one medium prismatic give it sharp, repeatable bursts, while its avionics tie directly into early-warning buoys. On border nets, “tachyon alert” means a fast mover has already cut the angle.

### Zeno `ZEN-400P`

Created for medium-range interdiction, the Zeno pairs a medium prismatic with economical rocket salvos to wear down fleeing targets. Modular hardpoints and easy field service made it a favorite of Citizen Guard patrols. Its name nods to pursuit paradoxes—once a Zeno starts the chase, the gap never seems to close for the prey.

### Darius `DR-300S`

Commissioned by the Heavy Systems Bureau to give citizen brigades a dependable lineholder, the Darius blends twin medium prismatics with stabilizing shields and guided missiles. It lacks flourish but excels at steady positional warfare, advancing behind a rational wall of data and fire. Crews respect it for what it is: the dependable center of an orderly advance.

### Cobalt `CLT-B1`

A precision striker from the Alloy Labs Initiative, the Cobalt mates a heavy energy cannon to a medium prismatic under reinforced shields. Its distinctive blue ceramic laminate isn’t for show—it dissipates heat and mitigates spall. Graduation units from several academies award “Cobalt keys” to pilots certified on its demanding fire-control regimen.

### Watcher `WAT-002`

Designed as a mesh-linked sentry, the Watcher stitches missiles, rockets, and prismatics into the battlespace network, shaping enemy movement rather than chasing it. When a Watcher is present, commanders talk about lanes and closures—the field becomes a diagram, and the diagram favors Orion.

### Deviance `DEV-2N`

The Deviance began as an unsanctioned field refit, where pilots overloaded a standard chassis with heavy prismatic arrays and rockets beyond recommended tolerances. Though the Assembly initially condemned the design, its brutal effectiveness in trials forced acceptance. Its name stuck, a reminder that sometimes progress comes from breaking rules.

### Xerxes `XER-200A`

Part of the Archives’ “Kings Series” naming convention, the Xerxes is an expeditionary spearhead with twin heavy prismatics, rockets, and resilient shields. It isn’t subtle—its job is to impose order at the point of contact. Citizen brigades treat it like a mobile verdict: the argument ends where Xerxes stands.

### Echelon `ECH-4R`

Built for charter enforcement and clean disables, the Echelon uses paired light prismatics and mixed missiles to peel weapons without catastrophic kills. Its telemetry suite prioritizes limb and mount solutions over cores. Echelons are routinely assigned to interdiction details, where compliance is required intact rather than destroyed.

### Blaze Bringer `BLZ-360K`

A field-illumination and suppression platform, the Blaze Bringer throws a curtain of coherent light—medium and light prismatics that dazzle sensors and scour cover. High sink capacity lets it sustain the show long after others cool off. Night operations doctrine calls it “turning darkness to glass.”

### Reckoner `REK-4M`

The Reckoner serves as an anchor and command relay. Heavy and medium prismatics handle threat nodes while a rocket bank shapes approaches. Its hardened comms and fire-control links allow it to coordinate allied units under sustained contact. Once established, a Reckoner position is intended to be held, not redeployed.

### Eradicator `ERAD-120R`

Despite the severe name, the Eradicator is a surgical siege tool. A heavy energy cannon and twin prismatics cut precise breach patterns while rockets clear debris and counter-pushes. City engineers like working behind it; the hole it makes is the hole the plan expected.

### Zealot `ZEL-20T`

Adopted enthusiastically by Citizen Guard auxiliaries, the Zealot layers shields and a full prismatic suite to hold ground with conviction. Rockets punish flanking attempts while heavy and medium beams keep pressure steady. Its “zeal” isn’t mysticism—just the civic habit of not giving an inch.

### Endermech `END-550M`

The Endermech serves as a versatile line-breaker, protected by advanced shielding. Armed with a heavy energy cannon, twin heavy prismatics, and missile support, it can adapt to almost any battlefield role. Pilots often remark on its uncanny precision, as if the machine anticipates threats a moment before they emerge.

### Markov `MKV-60L`

The Markov integrates predictive targeting from the Markov Kernel Project with twin heavy prismatics and deep heat reserves. Pilots joke it only needs to lock once; after that the future is already computed. In analytics drills, its variance charts are a thing of austere beauty.

### Rift Master `RFM-220C`

The Rift Master bends battlefields to its will. A chokepoint dominator, it seals narrow passes with heavy prismatic fire and rocket storms. Its shield lattice is tuned for glancing shots from extreme angles—perfect for canyon walls and megastructure corridors.

### Nebuchadnezzar `NEB-Z001`

A prestige fortress platform from the Archives’ “City-Builder” lineage, the Nebuchadnezzar layers heavy, medium, and light prismatics with compliant missiles under deep shields. It’s meant to hold capitals and corridors, projecting calm inevitability. The name fits: it builds order by unbuilding resistance.

### Aeon `AE-550X`

The Aeon is the pinnacle of Corporate technology, a mech designed to embody timeless supremacy. Quad heavy prismatics gave it unrivaled firepower, while advanced shields can make it feel invincible. Corporate propaganda has declared it ‘the machine that will outlast eternity.’
