# The Fringe Worlds

The Fringe Worlds are independent planetary states located along the outer edge of the Core. Though culturally and economically connected to the Core in varying degrees, these worlds are not formally governed by any of the Great Houses. Fringe governments range from stable republics and military protectorates to authoritarian regimes, mining syndicates, and merchant coalitions.

Unlike the Great Houses, Fringe Worlds generally lack the industrial capacity and logistical infrastructure required to maintain large interstellar fleets. Most possess only small defensive spacecraft intended for local patrol, customs enforcement, or orbital defense. Their military power is instead concentrated on the defense of individual planets through planetary militias, mech forces, fixed defenses, and localized aerospace units.

While no single Fringe World could rival the military power of a Great House, many are nevertheless formidable within their own systems. Invasion of a major Fringe World would often prove costly and politically difficult, especially given the limited strategic value of many frontier regions. As a result the Great Houses typically prefer diplomacy, trade agreements, economic pressure, or indirect influence rather than direct military conquest.

Life within the Fringe is generally harsher and less predictable than within the central Core. Infrastructure is often older and less reliable, while economic conditions vary dramatically from world to world. Frontier farming colonies, mining settlements, refinery towns, and isolated industrial outposts are common. Civilian populations frequently rely on local militias and mercenary contracts for protection against raiders and pirates.

Piracy is significantly more common within Fringe space. Numerous pirate clans, raider groups, smuggling networks, and criminal organizations operate from hidden bases on remote moons, asteroid fields, and isolated systems. Trade convoys traveling through the Fringe often require armed escorts, while many smaller settlements maintain extensive underground shelters and defensive fortifications.

Mercenary companies play an important role throughout the Fringe. Many independent worlds lack the manpower to maintain large standing armies and instead rely on mercenary units to supplement local defense forces. Successful mercenary commanders can become highly influential figures on Fringe Worlds, occasionally acting as military advisors, governors, or even political leaders.

The Fringe also serves as a gateway to the Frontier beyond. Trade routes, exploration expeditions, salvage operations, and StarCom relay stations extend outward from Fringe space into increasingly isolated and poorly documented regions. Information beyond the Fringe becomes fragmented and unreliable, with many systems remaining only partially charted.

Culturally, Fringe populations often view themselves as more independent and self-reliant than the populations of the central Core. While many worlds maintain strong economic and cultural ties to the Great Houses, distrust of centralized authority is common. Frontier traditions, local militias, survivalism, and planetary identity tend to play a larger role in public life than within the highly urbanized Core worlds.

To many citizens of the Core, the Fringe represents both danger and opportunity. It is a region associated with fortune seekers, mercenary companies, smugglers, explorers, bounty hunters, and pioneers. Though less stable than the central Core, the Fringe remains one of the few regions of human space where individuals and organizations can still rapidly rise to power and influence.
