# High Energy Cannons

> *"There are elegant weapons. Then there are High Energy Cannons."*
>
> — Mech pilot saying

## Overview

High Energy Cannons, commonly abbreviated as **HECs**, are among the oldest and most destructive energy weapons still in widespread military service.

Unlike lasers and blasters, which prioritize efficiency and control, High Energy Cannons focus extraordinary amounts of energy through a narrow discharge channel and release it in a single devastating burst. The resulting beam is only partially stable, producing tremendous destructive power at the cost of heat generation, reactor strain, and efficiency.

Though crude by comparison to many modern energy weapons, HECs remain feared throughout the Core for one simple reason: few weapons can match their raw stopping power.

---

## Battlefield Role

High Energy Cannons are designed to destroy heavily armored targets.

A successful HEC strike can cripple lighter mechs and inflict catastrophic damage against assault-class opponents. Their immense firepower makes them popular among breakthrough units, assault formations, and heavy combat mechs expected to engage enemy front-line forces directly.

The weapon's primary drawback is heat. Every discharge places enormous strain upon a mech's cooling systems, forcing pilots to carefully manage firing cycles during prolonged engagements.

Several specialized variants have emerged over the centuries. The **Long-Range HEC**, fielded exclusively by the Helios Sovereignty, sacrifices efficiency for extended engagement ranges and reflects the Sovereignty's preference for precision long-range warfare. The **Heavy HEC**, employed primarily by the Starcrest Protectorate, further increases the weapon's destructive power at the cost of additional heat, weight, and instability. These oversized cannons are most commonly found on the Protectorate's heaviest assault mechs.

## History

Most historians believe High Energy Cannons date back to the earliest generations of mech warfare.

While modern energy weapons have undergone countless refinements over the centuries, the fundamental principles behind the HEC have changed remarkably little. At its core, the weapon remains an elegant solution to a simple military problem: how to deliver the maximum possible amount of energy to a target in the shortest possible time.

Because of this simplicity, HECs survived technological collapses, political upheavals, and the Fall of the Empire largely unchanged. Even today, engineers often describe them as one of the purest expressions of energy weapon design ever created.

Every major military power fields standard HECs, though several factions have developed their own specialized variants. The Sovereignty's Long-Range HEC and the Protectorate's Heavy HEC are the most famous examples, each reflecting the battlefield philosophies of the nations that employ them.

For thousands of years, countless weapons have risen and fallen from favor. The High Energy Cannon endures because destruction, in its simplest form, rarely goes out of style.
