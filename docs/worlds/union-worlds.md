# Major Worlds of the Confederate Vanguard Union

> *"Forged from Victory."*
>
> — Motto of House Hawkins

The Confederate Vanguard Union is the largest military power in the Core. Ruled by House Hawkins from Starhold, the Union values discipline, service, duty, and collective strength above individual ambition.

Its citizens are taught that civilization survives only because someone is willing to defend it. Military service is respected throughout society, and many of the Union's institutions are organized around principles of merit, discipline, and public duty.

The Union fields the largest military force among the Great Houses and is widely regarded as the most dangerous conventional military power in human space.

## Starhold

**Population:** 2.9 Billion

Capital of the Confederate Vanguard Union and seat of House Hawkins.

Starhold serves as the political and military heart of the Union. Massive government complexes, military headquarters, and command academies dominate much of the planet's surface.

Virtually every major military campaign conducted by the Union is planned from Starhold.

To its citizens, Starhold represents unity, duty, and strength.

---

## Fortuna

**Population:** 3.6 Billion

The most populous world in the Union.

Fortuna is an industrial giant whose factories, shipyards, and manufacturing complexes help sustain the Union's enormous military.

Entire continents are devoted to industrial production, making Fortuna one of the most productive worlds in the Core.

Many citizens proudly refer to it as "the Forge of Victory."

---

## Hammerfall

**Population:** 800 Million

A heavily industrialized world dedicated to military production.

Weapons, ammunition, armor systems, and military hardware are manufactured in staggering quantities across Hammerfall's vast factory districts.

The world has become synonymous with the Union's war-making capacity.

---

## Galvin's Forge

**Population:** 500 Million

One of the oldest industrial worlds in Union territory.

Named after a legendary military leader, Galvin's Forge specializes in heavy manufacturing, armored vehicle production, and strategic materials processing.

Its foundries burn day and night.

---

## New Ramos

**Population:** 1.9 Billion

One of the Union's primary agricultural worlds.

Vast farming regions stretch across much of the planet's surface, producing food that supports both civilian populations and military deployments throughout Union territory.

New Ramos demonstrates that even the largest armies ultimately depend upon stable food production.

---

## Daxton's Dominion

**Population:** 1.8 Billion

The military heartland of the Union.

Training facilities, officer academies, military colleges, and recruitment centers dominate much of the world.

Many of the Union's senior officers began their careers on Daxton's Dominion.

Military service is deeply embedded within local culture.

## Freedom

→ See: [Freedom](../union/freedom/)

**Population:** 300 Million

A harsh penal world where the Union sends its most dangerous criminals, insurgents, deserters, failed officials, and political dissidents.

Sentencing to Freedom is permanent. Those exiled there forfeit their citizenship, titles, inheritance, and legal ties to their former lives. Once transferred to Freedom, an individual belongs to the world for life and serves the Union through one of two paths: labor or military service.

Life on Freedom is deliberately unforgiving. Vast penal settlements, labor camps, military compounds, and fortified detention zones dominate much of the planet's habitable regions. Beyond the walls lies a hostile wilderness filled with dangerous native fauna and environmental hazards that make escape nearly impossible.

Freedom is also one of the Union's most valuable resource worlds. Its mines produce vast quantities of strategic minerals and industrial materials under brutal conditions that claim countless lives each year. Those not assigned to the mines serve within the Freedom Corps, a harsh military organization recruited from the planet's exile population.

Though often dismissed by regular military personnel as little more than criminals in uniform, the Freedom Corps has earned a reputation for fanatic discipline, extreme casualty tolerance, and unwavering loyalty. Deployed primarily along the frontier, its soldiers are expected to endure hardships and losses that would break most conventional units. Throughout Union history, a handful of Freedom Corps commanders have risen from exile to become celebrated heroes of the Union.

The Union maintains Freedom not merely as a prison, but as a warning. Throughout Union space, the threat of exile to Freedom serves as a powerful deterrent against rebellion, sedition, desertion, and serious crime.

## Mercer's Gate

**Population:** 600 Million

One of the most heavily fortified worlds in Union territory.

Strategically positioned along critical border regions, Mercer's Gate serves as a defensive bulwark against external threats and internal unrest. Massive fortress complexes, military bases, orbital defense platforms, and hardened command centers cover much of the planet.

The world also houses major weapons factories, military training facilities, and logistics infrastructure that support Union military operations across nearby sectors.

Mercer's Gate is often described by Union strategists as the shield of the Union—a world built for war and prepared to withstand siege.

---

## Liberty's End

**Population:** 2.4 Billion

One of the most prosperous and independent worlds within the Confederate Vanguard Union.

Known for its sprawling metropolitan centers, advanced infrastructure, and thriving technological sector, Liberty's End enjoys a level of local autonomy uncommon within the highly centralized Union. Its citizens take pride in their traditions of self-governance and civic independence while remaining loyal members of the Union.

The planet's universities, research institutions, and technology firms contribute significantly to Union economic and military development. Many advanced systems used throughout Union space originated on Liberty's End.

Though culturally distinct from many of the Union's more militarized worlds, Liberty's End remains a valuable and influential member of the Union, providing both innovation and resources to support Union strength.

## The Union Character

The worlds of the Confederate Vanguard Union are united by a belief that strength creates stability.

Its citizens view military service as a responsibility rather than a burden. Public institutions emphasize merit, discipline, and collective achievement.

To critics, the Union can appear rigid and militaristic.

To its supporters, it represents humanity's best defense against chaos.

The Union does not apologize for its military power.

Its leaders believe peace is maintained not through hope or diplomacy, but through the certainty that aggression will be met with overwhelming force.
