# CoreNet

CoreNet is the primary interstellar communications and media network used throughout civilized human space. Maintained through a vast system of relay stations, orbital transmitters, planetary hubs, and StarCom communication infrastructure, CoreNet allows the rapid distribution of news, propaganda, entertainment, military reports, financial data, and civilian communications across much of the Core.

Although often described as a single unified network, CoreNet is in reality a fragmented collection of regional systems maintained by governments, corporations, StarCom installations, and private communication providers. The central Core worlds possess the most advanced and reliable access, while communication quality and transmission speed degrade significantly farther into the Fringe and Frontier.

For most civilians CoreNet serves as the primary source of information about events beyond their homeworlds. News broadcasts, political commentary, military updates, entertainment programming, and House propaganda are consumed daily across the Core. Most major governments maintain extensive media divisions dedicated to shaping public perception through carefully managed reporting and patriotic broadcasts.

Political propaganda is a major feature of CoreNet culture. The Great Houses regularly accuse one another of corruption, instability, aggression, or economic exploitation through official broadcasts and media campaigns. Frontier incidents, pirate attacks, border disputes, and military operations are frequently framed in ways favorable to each House’s political interests.

Mercenary companies also maintain a substantial presence across the network. Popular mercenary commanders, mech pilots, and military units often become celebrities through interviews, combat footage, sponsorship deals, and public recruitment campaigns. Arena combat broadcasts and military exhibitions are among the most widely viewed entertainment programs within many regions of the Core.

Despite its importance, CoreNet is not fully centralized or universally accessible. Transmission delays become increasingly severe farther from the Core due to the limitations of relay infrastructure and the dangers of maintaining communication routes through unstable frontier regions. Some distant systems may receive updates weeks or months after events occur within the central Core.

Censorship and information control vary considerably between regions and political powers. Certain governments heavily regulate local media and restrict outside broadcasts, while others permit relatively open information exchange. During periods of political crisis or military conflict, emergency restrictions and communication blackouts are common.

The Frontier remains only loosely connected to CoreNet. Many distant settlements possess limited or outdated communication infrastructure, while some isolated worlds operate entirely independent local networks. Information arriving from deep frontier space is often fragmented, unreliable, or heavily distorted by rumor and myth.

StarCom plays a critical role in maintaining long-distance communication infrastructure throughout human space. Many remote relay stations and frontier communication hubs depend entirely on StarCom maintenance and logistical support. As a result, StarCom possesses enormous influence over the flow of information between the Core, Fringe, and Frontier.

Beyond simple communication, CoreNet functions as one of the primary cultural bonds holding the Core together. Citizens separated by vast interstellar distances still consume many of the same news broadcasts, propaganda campaigns, sporting events, and entertainment programs. In this way CoreNet helps sustain a shared sense of civilization across a fragmented and declining human empire.
