# Minor Mercenary Units

> *"Every Great Company started as a small contract and a handful of steel."*
>
> — Mercenary saying

Minor Mercenary Units comprise the overwhelming majority of registered mercenary organizations operating throughout the Core.

While they lack the fame and political influence of the Greater Companies and Lesser Companies, minor units form the backbone of the mercenary profession. Numbering in the hundreds, these organizations undertake everything from frontier security and anti-piracy operations to convoy escort, reconnaissance, garrison duty, and local military contracts.

A typical minor unit ranges in size from a single company to a reinforced ward, though exact strength varies considerably. Some operate only a handful of mechs supported by vehicles and infantry, while others field substantial combined-arms formations capable of conducting independent campaigns.

The term *minor* should not be interpreted as insignificant. Many minor units possess long histories, respected commanders, and distinguished combat records. Several are among the most recognizable military organizations in the regions where they operate. The distinction is primarily one of scale. Compared to the Greater Companies, whose forces rival the militaries of Great Houses, and the Lesser Companies, which field armies measured in multiple wards, most minor units remain relatively small and specialized.

For many mercenaries, service within a minor unit represents the professional heart of the industry. These organizations are large enough to maintain dedicated logistics, training, and command structures, yet small enough that reputation, leadership, and battlefield performance remain intensely personal.

Many of today's most famous mercenary commanders, company founders, and Greater Companies trace their origins to a minor unit that survived long enough to earn a name.
