    Space Warfare

    “Ground wars can be rebuilt. Fleet wars are remembered forever.”
    — Core naval doctrine saying

    Overview
    Primary Theater Orbital and Interstellar Space
    Major Fleet Types Destroyers, Cruisers, Battleships, Battlestars
    Manufacturing Status Largely Lost Technology
    Strategic Function Deterrence and Force Projection
    Conflict Frequency Extremely Rare
    Common Strategic Reality “Every Ship Is Precious”

    All major powers within the Core maintain formidable starship fleets.

    These fleets include: - destroyers - cruisers - battleships - carriers - the dreaded battlestars

    Yet despite their immense military power, large-scale fleet warfare is exceedingly rare in the modern era.

    Unlike most conventional military equipment, major warships are extraordinarily difficult — and in many cases effectively impossible — to replace.

    Much of the advanced knowledge required for constructing large military vessels has been lost over centuries of decline, fragmentation, and technological stagnation.

    As a result, many of the largest ships in service today are ancient relics maintained through generations of painstaking repair and preservation.

    Throughout the Core, fleets are viewed less as expendable military assets and more as irreplaceable pillars of civilization itself.

    The Ancient Fleets

    Most major fleets operating in the modern Core trace their origins back centuries, with some vessels believed to predate even the Great Restoration.

    Many battleships and battlestars currently in service are: - ancient war relics - heavily reconstructed hulls - inherited fleet assets - restored pre-collapse vessels

    In numerous cases, modern engineers no longer fully understand all systems operating within the ships they maintain.

    Entire generations of naval engineers dedicate their lives to preserving fleet functionality through: - salvage - repair - reverse engineering - component cannibalization - ritualized maintenance traditions

    Because complete reconstruction of major warships is often impossible, even damaged vessels may remain in drydock for decades awaiting restoration.

    Fleet Manufacturing

    Although smaller spacecraft can still be produced throughout the Core, true capital ship manufacturing has become extraordinarily rare.

    Modern shipyards primarily exist to: - maintain existing fleets - repair damaged vessels - retrofit aging systems - recover battle damage - preserve ancient ship infrastructure

    Constructing entirely new capital-class warships requires: - industrial capability - material infrastructure - reactor engineering - fabrication systems - lost technical knowledge

    that few factions fully possess.

    This technological decline has fundamentally shaped modern naval doctrine.

    Unlike earlier eras, fleets cannot simply replace losses through industrial expansion.

    A destroyed battleship may represent a permanent strategic loss lasting generations.

    Major Fleet Classes

    Destroyers

    Destroyers are relatively fast and maneuverable warships used for: - escort duty - screening operations - patrol missions - anti-fighter defense - rapid response operations

    Most modern destroyers are among the few larger warships still produced in limited numbers.

    Cruisers

    Cruisers form the backbone of most fleet formations.

    They balance: - firepower - durability - operational range - troop transport capability

    Cruisers frequently serve as: - command vessels - fleet anchors - expeditionary platforms

    during military deployments.

    Battleships

    Battleships are among the oldest and most heavily armed vessels still operating within the Core.

    These immense warships are designed for: - fleet engagements - orbital dominance - strategic deterrence

    Many battleships possess immense symbolic importance beyond their military value.

    The destruction of a major battleship can become a civilization-defining political event.

    Battlestars

    Battlestars are the largest and most feared military vessels in known space.

    These colossal warships combine: - fleet command capability - heavy weapons systems - aerospace deployment - troop transport - long-term operational endurance

    A single battlestar may project enough military power to dominate entire sectors.

    Fortunately, true battlestars are extraordinarily rare.

    Many analysts believe no modern power fully understands how to construct new ones.

    Why Fleet Warfare Is Rare

    Large-scale naval conflict is uncommon throughout the Core for a simple reason:

    fleet losses are often irreplaceable.

    Even victorious fleet engagements can cripple a faction strategically for decades.

    As a result, modern fleets are used primarily for: - deterrence - intimidation - force projection - transport - blockade operations - strategic presence

    rather than direct annihilation.

    Fleet commanders are generally highly cautious regarding escalation.

    Among naval officers, there is a common saying:

    “A reckless admiral burns centuries in a single hour.”

    Orbital Bombardment

    Planetary bombardment is extremely rare throughout civilized space.

    Several factors contribute to this restraint.

    Cultural Stigma

    The Core possesses a deeply ingrained cultural hostility toward weapons of mass destruction.

    Many citizens regard indiscriminate orbital bombardment as: - barbaric - dishonorable - existentially dangerous

    This stigma dates back centuries and remains deeply embedded in military doctrine.

    Political Consequences

    Open planetary bombardment is generally viewed as an outright declaration of unrestricted war.

    The Star Regency would almost certainly condemn any faction engaging in unauthorized mass bombardment.

    Such actions could potentially result in: - expulsion from the Conclave - political isolation - coordinated retaliation - rogue state designation

    Few factions are willing to risk such consequences.

    Strategic Inefficiency

    Contrary to popular belief, orbital bombardment is often militarily inefficient.

    Although devastating against: - cities - infrastructure - fixed installations

    bombardment is far less effective against mobile mech formations capable of dispersal and maneuver.

    Precision targeting from orbit also remains difficult under many battlefield conditions.

    Most powers prefer to capture valuable infrastructure rather than destroy it outright.

    In an age where advanced technology itself is increasingly precious, annihilation is often viewed as wasteful.

    Mechs and Naval Warfare

    Although fleets dominate space itself, mechs remain central to planetary warfare.

    Modern military doctrine generally views fleets as tools for: - transport - deployment - orbital control - strategic movement

    while mechs determine the outcome of ground campaigns.

    This distinction has shaped warfare throughout the modern era.

    A fleet may seize orbit.

    But holding a world still requires steel on the ground.

    The Great Houses and Fleet Doctrine

    Each major faction maintains distinct naval traditions.

    Confederate Vanguard Union

    The Union favors: - large coordinated fleet formations - aggressive strategic positioning - heavy logistical mobilization

    Helios Sovereignty

    The Sovereignty emphasizes: - prestige fleets - ceremonial formations - political projection - highly visible naval presence

    Omnisphere Imperium

    The Imperium maintains some of the oldest surviving fleets in the Core.

    Imperium naval doctrine values: - endurance - discipline - fleet preservation - overwhelming retaliation

    Fire symbolism remains deeply integrated into many Imperium naval traditions.

    Starcrest Protectorate

    The Protectorate focuses heavily upon: - defensive fleet posture - territorial security - fortified orbital networks - attritional fleet doctrine

    Orion Corporate

    Orion naval strategy emphasizes: - advanced systems integration - precision targeting - electronic warfare - sensor superiority - fleet coordination

    Civilian Perception

    To ordinary citizens throughout the Core, fleets possess an almost mythical status.

    Most people will never directly encounter: - battleships - battlestars - major fleet formations

    outside of propaganda broadcasts or ceremonial displays.

    Many ancient warships are treated with near-religious reverence by their crews.

    Some vessels possess: - centuries of recorded combat history - famous commanders - legendary reputations - political symbolism

    far beyond their battlefield roles.

    Modern Outlook

    As tensions rise throughout the Core, concerns regarding fleet escalation continue to grow.

    Many analysts fear that if a major inter-house naval war ever truly erupted, the resulting losses could permanently cripple civilization itself.

    Others argue this very fear is what continues preventing such wars from occurring.

    For now, the fleets endure.

    Ancient steel drifting between the stars — maintained by generations who no longer fully understand the machines they preserve, yet still trust them to hold civilization together.
