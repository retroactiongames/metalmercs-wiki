# History of the Core

> *“History is not memory. History is what survives.”*  
> — StarCom Historical Directorate

## :material-timeline-clock: Historical Overview

| Era | Common Description |
|---|---|
| The Age of Empire | The ancient united civilization of humanity |
| The Collapse | The fall of the Empire and beginning of the dark age |
| The Dark Age | Occupation, fragmentation, and civilizational decline |
| The Great Restoration | Return of the Regent and reclamation of the Core |
| The Modern Era | Rise of the Great Houses and the Stellar Conclave |

The history of the Core is fragmented, contradictory, and often mythologized.

Most surviving records originate only from the period following the Great Restoration. Earlier eras are known primarily through damaged archives, oral traditions, military legends, religious stories, and reconstructed StarCom historical records.

As a result, much of early Core history remains heavily disputed among historians.

Some scholars argue that many commonly accepted events may have been exaggerated, altered, or entirely fabricated over centuries of instability and record loss.

Nevertheless, a broad historical framework is generally accepted throughout civilized space.

## The Age of Empire

Long before the modern Great Houses existed, the Core is believed to have been united beneath a vast interstellar civilization commonly referred to simply as *the Empire*.

Some surviving traditions instead describe this period as:
- the Old Empire
- the First Empire
- the Golden Age
- the Golden Empire

No universally accepted name survives.

According to surviving accounts, the Empire controlled nearly all known Core space and possessed technological capabilities surpassing those of the modern era.

Stories from this period describe:
- massive fleets
- highly advanced scientific knowledge
- stable interstellar governance
- immense industrial worlds
- near-unified human civilization

Very little verifiable evidence from this age survives intact.

Modern historians continue to debate how technologically advanced the Empire truly was, or whether many surviving stories have become exaggerated through centuries of retelling.

## The Collapse

At some point in the distant past, the Empire collapsed.

Most surviving traditions associate this collapse with the mysterious Ophidian Supremacy.

Descriptions of the Supremacy vary dramatically between surviving records.

Some accounts describe:
- an external invading civilization
- non-human conquerors
- entities emerging from deep space
- an empire from beyond the frontier

Other interpretations argue the Ophidians may have been:
- rival human powers
- a coalition of rebellious systems
- a mythologized interpretation of civilizational collapse itself
- political propaganda invented during the Restoration era

No verified evidence conclusively confirms the true nature of the Ophidian Supremacy.

What is generally accepted is that the Empire fell during a catastrophic period of warfare and instability that shattered interstellar civilization throughout the Core.

## The Dark Age

Following the Collapse, the Core entered a prolonged dark age.

Large portions of interstellar infrastructure failed or were abandoned. Many worlds became isolated from one another for generations. Scientific knowledge declined, centralized governance disappeared, and countless historical records were lost forever.

This period remains one of the least understood eras of human history.

Many regions devolved into:
- isolated planetary states
- warlord territories
- pirate enclaves
- collapsed industrial systems
- fragmented frontier societies

Entire worlds vanished from surviving records.

Even basic interstellar travel became dangerous and unreliable across many regions.

## Survivors of the Dark Age

While much of civilization collapsed, several powers survived in diminished forms.

### The Omnisphere Imperium

The Omnisphere Imperium is the only modern Great House widely acknowledged to have survived continuously from the time of the Empire itself.

Though the Imperium lost significant territory during the Collapse, its core worlds successfully endured through a combination of defensive isolation, fortified infrastructure, and strict internal control.

Imperium records claim their ancestors resisted the Ophidian Supremacy directly during the final years of the Empire.

Because of this continuity, the Imperium often presents itself as the oldest legitimate civilization remaining within the Core.

### The Starcrest Protectorate

The origins of the modern Starcrest Protectorate trace back to a larger pre-Collapse polity that did not survive the dark age intact.

The Protectorate itself emerged from the final defensive frontier regions of this fallen power.

Though greatly diminished, these surviving frontier defense forces managed to preserve their territory throughout the collapse and gradually expanded in strength over subsequent centuries.

This legacy continues to shape Protectorate culture, which strongly emphasizes resilience, sacrifice, and defensive duty.

### The Orion Cooperative

The organization that would eventually become Orion Corporate first emerged during the dark age as a defensive alliance between three isolated worlds attempting to survive the widespread instability of the era.

By pooling:
- scientific expertise
- industrial production
- technological research
- defensive resources

the Cooperative succeeded in preserving relative stability while much of the surrounding Core deteriorated.

Their intense focus on technological advancement developed largely as a reaction against the collapse they witnessed around them.

Over time, their superior technology and organizational efficiency allowed them to expand their influence substantially.

## The Exile

According to Restoration-era records, remnants of the Empire’s military forces survived the Collapse by fleeing beyond known Core space into the deep fringe.

Among these exiled survivors was the bloodline of House Caledon.

Very little verified information exists regarding the centuries spent in exile.

Some historians question whether the exile narratives are entirely literal, while others believe the returning forces may have originated from hidden frontier territories rather than true extra-Core exile.

Regardless of their origins, the return of the Caledon line would permanently reshape human civilization.

## The Great Restoration

Several centuries after the Collapse, the exiled Regent and the surviving Vanguard military forces returned to the Core during the conflict now known as the Great Restoration.

By this period, the mysterious Ophidian Supremacy had either vanished entirely or fragmented beyond recognition.

The returning Vanguard forces rapidly reclaimed much of the Core under the leadership of House Caledon.

The Great Restoration marked the rebirth of large-scale interstellar civilization.

Following the Restoration:
- trade routes were re-established
- jump networks stabilized
- centralized governance returned
- industrial systems recovered
- communication networks expanded
- surviving worlds were reunited

Most surviving modern historical records begin during this era.

## The Rise of the Great Houses

The modern Great Houses largely emerged during the decades following the Great Restoration.

### The Confederate Vanguard Union

The Confederate Vanguard Union originated directly from the Regent’s Vanguard military forces during the Restoration.

As the returning armies reclaimed vast territories throughout the Core, House Caledon required a stable governing authority capable of administering these newly restored regions.

Statehood was therefore granted to the Vanguard military administration, eventually forming the Confederate Vanguard Union.

This origin continues to shape the Union’s identity as both a military state and a guardian of restored civilization.

### The Helios Sovereignty

The Helios Sovereignty emerged from the merchant houses, navigators, administrators, and economic coordinators who played a central role in rebuilding interstellar trade and civil infrastructure following the Restoration.

House Caledon relied heavily upon these organizations to:
- restore safe travel
- stabilize commerce
- rebuild economic systems
- coordinate reconstruction
- re-establish interstellar governance

Through this process, the ruling Houses Aerin and Payne accumulated enormous wealth and political influence.

In modern centuries, critics occasionally argue the Sovereignty has drifted away from its original reconstruction ideals toward aristocratic excess and political elitism.

Nevertheless, Helios remains one of the primary stabilizing economic powers within the Core.

Members of Houses Aerin and Payne frequently serve as the Voice of the Regent — a highly prestigious office responsible for coordinating much of the practical governance of the Core Worlds.

### Orion Corporate

Following the Restoration, the technologically advanced Orion Cooperative aligned itself closely with the returning Regency.

Its reliability, industrial coordination, and scientific capabilities earned it increasing territorial authority and political influence.

Over time, the Cooperative evolved into the modern Orion Corporate.

## The Modern Era

The current era began following the establishment of:
- the Star Regency
- the Stellar Conclave
- the Great Houses
- StarCom
- the restored Core trade networks

For several centuries, the Core has remained relatively stable compared to the chaos of the dark age.

However, tensions between the Great Houses continue to intensify.

Political rivalry, frontier instability, piracy, mercenary conflicts, industrial competition, and ideological division increasingly threaten the balance established during the Restoration.

Many historians believe the modern Core now stands at a critical turning point.

Whether civilization enters a new golden age or repeats the mistakes that once destroyed the Empire remains unknown.
