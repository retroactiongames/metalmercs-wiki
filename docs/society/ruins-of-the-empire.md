# Ruins of the Empire

Scattered across the Core are the remnants of the ancient Empire, vast abandoned regions left behind following centuries of decline, depopulation, economic collapse, and political fragmentation. Though the modern Core remains highly civilized and technologically advanced, much of humanity now occupies only a fraction of the infrastructure once maintained during the height of Imperial rule.

On many worlds, modern cities stand beside or atop far older urban centers dating back to the Empire. Massive abandoned districts, sealed transit systems, forgotten industrial sectors, and derelict arcologies are common even on wealthy Core planets. Entire metropolitan regions may stand empty for hundreds of kilometers beyond the populated zones of modern cities.

The Empire possessed a far larger population than the modern Core and maintained extensive interstellar infrastructure across thousands of systems. During its height, planetary populations reached levels that many modern worlds could no longer sustain following the collapse of centralized authority and the decline of interstellar trade networks. Over time, populations consolidated around defensible and economically viable urban centers while countless peripheral settlements were abandoned.

Many ruins remain dangerous and poorly understood. Automated defense systems, unstable infrastructure, toxic industrial contamination, and unexplored subterranean sectors continue to pose hazards centuries after abandonment. Some ancient transit systems and utility networks still function autonomously despite no longer being fully understood by modern engineers.

Imperial ruins are especially common beneath the largest cities of the Core. Vast underground levels, abandoned maglev tunnels, forgotten military bunkers, and sealed industrial complexes often lie beneath modern population centers. In some cities entire lower districts have been declared permanently off-limits due to structural instability, criminal occupation, or unexplained system failures.

Salvage operations within Imperial ruins represent a major industry throughout the Core and Fringe alike. Lost technology, industrial components, military hardware, and archival data recovered from ancient facilities can command enormous value. Many mercenary companies supplement their income through salvage contracts, escort duties, and ruin security operations.

The Great Houses frequently conduct classified expeditions into major Imperial sites, particularly former research installations, shipyards, and military facilities. Rumors persist of hidden Imperial weapons caches, sealed artificial intelligences, lost colonies, and experimental technologies buried within forgotten sectors of the old Empire.

For ordinary citizens the ruins of the Empire are both mundane and mythic. Most Core civilians live among Imperial infrastructure in some form, using transportation systems, reactors, or industrial machinery whose origins long predate modern civilization. At the same time the deeper ruins remain objects of fascination, superstition, and fear. Stories of hidden vaults, lost riches, rogue defense systems, and vanished expeditions are common throughout Core media and frontier folklore.

The existence of the ruins serves as a constant reminder that modern civilization survives in the shadow of a greater age. Humanity did not inherit an expanding empire, but the remnants of one already fallen.
