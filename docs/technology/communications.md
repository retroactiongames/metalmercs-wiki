# Communications

> *"Information travels faster than light. Civilization travels at the speed of infrastructure."*
>
> — StarCom Communications Manual

## Overview

Interstellar communication throughout the Core relies upon vast networks of communication stations, relay buoys, orbital facilities, and starships that continuously rebroadcast data between systems.

Using the same exotic physics that make jump travel possible, communication systems can transmit data faster than light across interstellar distances. However, these signals degrade rapidly over distance and cannot simply be broadcast across the galaxy from a single transmitter.

As a result, communication speed depends heavily upon the availability and reliability of relay networks.

## Relay Networks

A transmission sent from one world rarely reaches its destination directly.

Instead, communications are transmitted from one relay station to another, with each station receiving, amplifying, verifying, and rebroadcasting the signal onward.

A typical message traveling between major Core worlds may pass through dozens of relay stations before reaching its final destination.

These relay networks form the backbone of modern civilization.

Without them, interstellar communication would become impractically slow or impossible.

## Faster-Than-Light Transmission

Unlike ancient electromagnetic communications, modern interstellar communications utilize FTL transmission technology derived from the same mathematical breakthroughs that enable jump drives.

However, FTL communication is not unlimited.

Signals gradually weaken and accumulate distortion as they travel. Beyond a certain distance, a transmission becomes indistinguishable from background noise and can no longer be reliably decoded.

To overcome this limitation, relay stations continuously receive, reconstruct, and rebroadcast messages throughout known space.

The result is a vast communication web spanning much of human civilization.

## The Great House Networks

Each of the Great Houses maintains extensive communication infrastructure throughout its territory.

Relay stations are commonly found on:

- inhabited worlds
- orbital facilities
- military outposts
- navigation buoys
- strategic jump points

Military fleets can also function as temporary communication nodes.

During major military operations, warships frequently establish relay chains that allow commanders to coordinate forces across multiple systems.

The destruction or disruption of these networks is considered a serious military threat.

## StarCom Communications

No organization possesses a larger communication network than StarCom.

Over centuries, StarCom has constructed and maintained thousands of relay stations, navigation beacons, communication buoys, survey outposts, and communication vessels throughout known space.

This infrastructure allows information to move between the Frontier and the Core with remarkable speed.

Under normal circumstances:

- Messages within the Core are delivered within a few days.
- Messages from most Frontier settlements reach the Core within several days.
- Remote Frontier regions may require up to a week.
- Exceptional circumstances can result in longer delays.

The StarCom network is widely regarded as one of the most important pieces of infrastructure in human space.

Many historians argue that StarCom's communications network is nearly as important as its navigation services.

## Communication Delays

Despite traveling faster than light, communication is not instantaneous.

Every relay introduces delays.

Stations require time to:

- receive transmissions
- verify signal integrity
- correct transmission errors
- route traffic
- rebroadcast messages

As a result, even routine communication may require days to reach its destination.

Citizens of the Core generally view such delays as normal.

A message sent across the Core often arrives in a timeframe comparable to physical mail services of earlier eras.

## Frontier Communication

Communication becomes increasingly difficult beyond established relay networks.

A vessel operating in remote space without access to relay stations may be effectively isolated from the wider galaxy.

Although a ship may possess powerful communication equipment, it cannot simply broadcast a message all the way back to the Core.

Without intermediate stations to receive and rebroadcast the signal, transmission quality eventually degrades beyond recovery.

This reality creates significant challenges for:

- explorers
- survey vessels
- mercenary companies
- Frontier colonies
- military patrols

Many remote settlements depend entirely upon periodic StarCom visits to remain connected to the wider human community.

## Courier Ships

For highly sensitive information, physical delivery remains common.

Encrypted data storage devices can be transported aboard ships, avoiding many of the interception risks associated with conventional communication networks.

Government agencies, military organizations, intelligence services, and major corporations frequently employ courier vessels for critical communications.

Such couriers may carry:

- diplomatic correspondence
- military orders
- intelligence reports
- financial records
- classified research

In some situations, a trusted courier is considered more secure than any communication network.

## Military Communications

Communication infrastructure is considered a strategic asset.

Military campaigns often revolve around securing or disrupting relay networks.

A commander who controls communication infrastructure can coordinate forces across multiple systems, while an isolated force may find itself effectively blind.

Many military planners view communications as equally important as fuel, ammunition, and logistics.

Entire offensives have succeeded or failed based upon the reliability of relay chains.

## The Information Age

Despite the immense distances separating human worlds, the relay networks maintained by the Great Houses and StarCom ensure that most citizens remain connected to the wider galaxy.

News, commerce, diplomacy, military orders, and personal correspondence flow continuously across known space.

Modern communication technology allows information to travel faster than light, but only where infrastructure exists to support it.

Beyond the reach of relay networks, even the most advanced civilizations can find themselves isolated.

In the modern era, communication is not limited by technology.

It is limited by the reach of civilization itself.
