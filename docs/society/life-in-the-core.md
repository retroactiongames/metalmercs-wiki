# Life in the Core

The Core is the heart of civilized human space and the center of political, economic, and cultural life. It occupies the territory once ruled by the ancient Empire, a civilization far larger and more populous than the modern Core. Though the great cities of the Core rival those of the Imperial age in safety and prosperity, much of the old Empire remains abandoned. Across even the wealthiest worlds, forgotten districts, sealed arcologies, and ruined industrial sectors stand as reminders of a greater age.

For most civilians, life in the Core is stable and relatively secure. The average citizen is far removed from the battlefields and frontier raids often depicted on the CoreNet. Millions work ordinary jobs as laborers, mechanics, engineers, administrators, merchants, medical personnel, and technicians. While standards of living vary between worlds and social classes, the central Core worlds are generally prosperous and orderly.

Daily life differs dramatically depending on location. Citizens of wealthy central worlds often live in towering urban centers supported by advanced infrastructure inherited from the Empire. High-speed transit systems, automated manufacturing, and vast commercial districts dominate these worlds. Outside the largest population centers however, signs of decline are common. Entire districts may stand abandoned for centuries, while old transit tunnels and industrial sectors remain sealed or forgotten beneath modern cities.

In the outer regions of the Core, life becomes harsher. Agricultural colonies, mining settlements, and frontier industrial worlds often require difficult manual labor and constant maintenance simply to survive. Equipment failures, supply shortages, and pirate activity are more common farther from the central worlds. In many regions mercenary companies supplement local House forces in defending civilian populations from raiders and criminal organizations.

Mercenaries occupy a unique place within Core culture. To many civilians they are viewed as larger-than-life figures, somewhere between soldiers, celebrities, and gladiators. Famous mercenary commanders and mech pilots are often featured in entertainment broadcasts, recruitment advertisements, and news coverage. Arena combat, military tournaments, and mech exhibitions attract enormous audiences across the Core. While many admire mercenaries for their skill and independence, others view them as dangerous opportunists motivated solely by profit.

Despite the relative safety of the Core, political tensions between the Great Houses are ever present. News broadcasts regularly feature reports of border incidents, trade disputes, sabotage accusations, pirate raids, and military exercises. Most citizens however experience these conflicts indirectly through propaganda, entertainment, and economic fluctuations rather than open warfare.

Beyond the authority of the Great Houses lie the Fringe Worlds and the distant Frontier. To many Core citizens these regions are viewed with a mixture of fascination and suspicion. Stories of pirates, frontier warlords, ancient ruins, and unexplored systems remain popular subjects throughout Core media and entertainment.

Though the modern Core remains powerful and civilized, it exists in the shadow of the Empire that came before. Humanity survives not through expansion, but through preservation. Much of daily life within the Core revolves around maintaining the immense infrastructure, technology, and institutions inherited from earlier ages.

## The Core Dream

Most citizens believe they live in the greatest civilization humanity has ever known.

Children are taught that the Great Houses restored order following the collapse of the Empire and safeguarded civilization from centuries of chaos.

This narrative contains elements of truth.

The Core is safer, wealthier, and more stable than most regions of known space.

Yet historians continue to debate whether modern society represents a restoration of Imperial civilization or merely its most successful surviving fragment.
