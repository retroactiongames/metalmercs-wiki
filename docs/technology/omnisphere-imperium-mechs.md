# Imperium Mechs

Imperium mech forces combine common Core platforms with fast, elegant, often serpentine machines built around mobility, opening volleys, and the doctrine of fierce beauty.

This roster includes both the common Core designs available to all major factions and the signature designs most closely associated with the Omnisphere Imperium.

## Common Core Designs

### Hornet `HN-120`

One of the oldest light designs still in circulation, the Hornet was cheap to manufacture and simple to maintain. Armed with twin light lasers, it was never meant to duel, but rather to scout, harass, and sting at vulnerable points before fleeing. Its proliferation across the Core is owed to its reliability and low cost—where there is conflict, Hornets are never far behind.

### Lynx `LNX-40E`

The Lynx became popular during the border wars for its balance of mobility and firepower. With paired medium lasers and a small rocket rack, it could serve as both flanker and support unit. Though newer mechs have surpassed it, the Lynx endures thanks to its rugged frame and versatility—qualities that made it a staple in every House’s arsenal.

### Outrider `OR-640`

Built for patrol duty and frontier policing, the Outrider combined endurance with straightforward weaponry. Its heavy and medium lasers gave it enough punch to deter raiders without requiring complex supply lines. While unimpressive by modern standards, the Outrider’s long service life and adaptability have ensured its presence in garrisons across the Core.

### Yeoman `YM-10F`

The Yeoman was designed as a dependable multi-role platform for Houses lacking specialized production. Its mixed loadout—autoguns, lasers, and scorchers—made it a jack-of-all-trades capable of handling infantry, armor, or other mechs in a pinch. Though rarely a commander’s first choice, the Yeoman’s steady performance earned it a reputation as the soldier’s friend.

### Lineman `LM-30C`

Heavy, slow, and built to endure punishment, the Lineman was conceived as a frontline brawler to hold the line while others maneuvered. Its vulkan cannon and paired autoguns provided steady suppressive fire. Though outclassed by newer heavies, it remains common wherever Houses need durable firepower to anchor defenses.

### Lancer `LNC-220`

The Lancer was among the first medium-heavies to emphasize precision energy fire over massed rockets. Armed with a heavy laser and layered support beams, it excelled at punching through weakened armor. Once hailed as a duelist’s machine, it is now more often used as a training platform or second-line support, though many veteran pilots still prize its accuracy.

### Longstrike `LG-210`

An aging missile platform, the Longstrike was originally deployed to provide sustained bombardment from behind the lines. With its paired racks and autogun backups, it could soften enemy positions before an assault. Though slow and vulnerable up close, its simplicity and ease of production have kept it in steady circulation across the Core.

### Champion `CH-70K`

Once built to serve in ceremonial tournaments as well as war, the Champion balanced missile fire with lasers and a vulkan. Though never as specialized as later duelists, it gained popularity as a reliable all-rounder. Many Houses still field Champions in honor guards or training battalions, where their long heritage and recognizable silhouette command respect.

### Bulldog `BD-770`

The Bulldog earned its name in countless sieges, battering defenses with high energy cannons and rocket salvos. Slow but stubborn, it excelled in drawn-out attrition battles. Even today, the Bulldog remains a favored choice for militias and second-line forces who need a hard-hitting mech that can take punishment and keep advancing.

### Marksman `MK-350`

The Marksman was designed as a fire-support chassis, mounting multiple vulkans for sustained barrages. Though criticized for its heat inefficiency and limited armor, it carved a niche as a battlefield “sweeper,” punishing light mechs and infantry that strayed too close. In modern times, it is often relegated to defensive roles, but its name still carries weight.

### Gladiator `GL-882`

A relic of the arena era, the Gladiator was built for spectacle as much as war. With rockets, vulkan, and lasers, it was capable of delivering flashy, punishing blows that made it a crowd favorite. Many Houses retired theirs to ceremonial duty, but in poorer regions of the Core, Gladiators still see combat, proving that even showpieces can draw blood.

### Kingsguard `KG-950`

The Kingsguard was once marketed as the ultimate “command mech,” a prestige unit to accompany noble lords into battle. Its missiles and mixed weaponry made it flexible, while its armor kept its pilot secure. Though time has dulled its reputation, the Kingsguard endures as a symbol of authority on the battlefield—still favored by militia leaders and House scions alike.


## Signature Imperium Designs

### Zephyr `ZPH-01`

A relic of the Imperium’s dueling traditions, the Zephyr was designed for blinding speed, able to outpace heavier mechs with ease. Armed with a blaster and light lasers, it darts in, strikes, and vanishes, leaving foes bewildered. Its name speaks to its nature: swift as wind, impossible to grasp.

### Drake `DRK-06`

The Drake was crafted in homage to the Imperium’s dragon symbology, its blaster and twin scorchers breathing fire across enemy lines. Lightly armored but terrifying up close, it thrives in ambushes and raids. Pilots often paint their machines with scaled patterns, reinforcing its image as a dragon reborn in steel.

### Ronin `RON-12`

A mech for warriors without masters, the Ronin was adopted by Imperium mercenary bands during the Old Imperium. With rockets for sudden fury and a blaster for dueling, it embodies the lone fighter’s creed: self-reliance, defiance, and a readiness to sell its blade to any cause.

### Pyrefox `PYX-53`

True to its name, the Pyrefox engulfs battlefields with blasters and fire weapons, making it a terror in close combat. Developed to cleanse rebellious enclaves, it carries both symbolic and practical weight—reminding enemies that resisting the Imperium will always end in flames.

### Incursor `ICR-99`

A reckless raider’s machine, the Incursor is fast, lightly protected, and dangerously over-armed for its frame. Rockets, blasters, and lasers make it unpredictable and deadly in the hands of bold pilots. Many Imperium aces built their reputations in Incursors, treating each battle as both gamble and performance.

### Jaguar `JGR-72`

Named for the fierce predator, the Jaguar embodies speed and ferocity. Its vulkan cannon and blaster combination allows it to tear through lighter foes, while its design prioritizes the swift, lethal strike over defense. In Imperium culture, Jaguars are seen as hunters born for war, and this mech carries that tradition.

### Ocelot `OCE-45`

The Ocelot was built to haunt enemies at range, its heavy energy cannon and blasters striking before foes can retaliate. Though lightly armored, its eerie precision and sudden appearances earned it a reputation as a phantom of the battlefield. Among Imperium warriors, piloting a Ocelot is considered an honor tied to ancestral rites of death and remembrance.

### Umbrantarch `UMB-39`

The Umbrantarch is a fast, fear-inducing strike mech armed with a heavy blaster and scorcher systems, optimized for repeated hit-and-run assaults. More than a combat platform, the Umbrantarch is regarded as a terror weapon—its silhouette emerging through fire and smoke like an omen. Umbrantarch pilots are said to walk with spirits, feared as much by allies as by enemies.

### Redeemer `RDM-10`

The Redeemer is a fast, hard-hitting medium platform designed for decisive strikes against exposed targets. Its rockets and blasters allow it to deliver sudden, concentrated fire before repositioning. Redeemers are deployed where momentum must be seized quickly and held through force.

### Nagara `NGA-47`

Built during an era of holy wars, the Nagara combined heavy firepower with symbolic weight. With its HEC and heavy Vulkan cannon, it was designed to break enemy champions and restore order. To this day, the Imperium frames Nagaras as machines of salvation through fire and steel.

### Drakkon `DRK-82`

A monstrous war machine, the Drakkon is drenched in flamethrowers and blasters, spewing destruction in every direction. It earned its name not from myth, but from its battlefield presence—unyielding, terrifying, and impossible to ignore. To the Imperium, the Drakkon is both dragon and executioner.

### Ordosmech `ORD-07`

The Ordosmech embodies the Imperium’s martial faith, built as a crusader’s machine. Armed with Vulkan cannons, rockets, and blasters, it balances mass firepower with religious symbolism. Pilots clad their Ordosmechs in heraldry and ritual markings, treating each battle as both ritual war and ancestral duty.

### Warclaw `WCL-61`

The Warclaw was commissioned during the Imperium’s early expansion campaigns, when rapid assaults proved insufficient against fortified resistance. Designed for prolonged engagements, it combines blasters, Vulkan cannons, and scorcher systems to apply constant pressure while advancing methodically. The Warclaw is deployed when territory must be taken and held, not merely breached.

### Nightwing `NGT-W4`

The Nightwing prowls battlefields like a shadow, unleashing rockets, flamethrowers, and blaster fire before fading back into darkness. Heavily armed for its size, it embodies the Imperium’s preference for intimidation as well as annihilation. Its name, like its silhouette, evokes wings spread across the night sky.

### Onagor `ONA-37`

The Onagor was built as a shock unit, carrying heavy vulkans and blasters to overwhelm enemies with sheer volume of fire. Though slower than some peers, it compensates with raw, thunderous destruction. Onagor is an ancient Imperium name, drawn from pre-unification myths describing a colossal entity whose advance was said to end wars by erasing the ground they were fought on.

### Praetorian `PRA-97`

The Praetorian is one of the Imperium’s most feared and recognizable war machines. With paired heavy energy cannons, blasters, and scorchers, it combines versatile firepower with an imposing silhouette that dominates the field. More than a frontline brawler, the Praetorian is a commander’s mech, deployed to anchor formations and inspire dread in equal measure.

### Neomech `NEO-60`

Born of experiments in massed rocket fire, the Neomech is a heavy line unit that drowns enemies in salvos while its blasters and lasers cut down survivors. Its name reflects both its modernity and its connection to the Imperium’s constant cycle of rebirth: old forms reforged into new engines of war.

### Star Slayer `SLY-10`

The Star Slayer represents the Imperium’s mythic ambition. With heavy energy cannons and blasters, it was designed as a “god-killer,” capable of standing against the mightiest machines of rival Houses. Its name is both boast and prophecy—declaring that no star, no throne, and no rival shines brighter than the Imperium.
