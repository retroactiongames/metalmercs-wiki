# Technology

From the jump drives that connect distant stars to the communications networks that carry information between worlds, modern society depends upon an immense web of machinery, infrastructure, and scientific knowledge accumulated over thousands of years.

Although humanity remains technologically advanced by almost any standard, much of its most important technology originates from earlier eras. Many systems are maintained, refined, and reproduced without being fully understood. Engineers can repair them, operators can use them, and manufacturers can build replacements, yet the deeper scientific principles behind some technologies remain the subject of ongoing research and debate.

This reliance upon inherited knowledge has shaped every aspect of modern civilization. The Great Houses compete not only through military strength and political influence, but through control of industrial worlds, research institutions, shipyards, communications networks, and manufacturing centers.

## Major Technology in the Modern Core

Several technological systems form the backbone of interstellar civilization:

* **[Jump Drives](jump-drive.md)** allow faster-than-light travel between star systems.
* **[Communication Networks](communications.md)** transmit information across vast distances through relay infrastructure.
* **[Industrial Infrastructure](industry.md)** produces the ships, vehicles, weapons, and equipment required to sustain civilization.
* **[Space Warfare Systems](space-warfare.md)** project military power between worlds and protect vital trade routes.
* **[Weapons Technology](weapons/)** provides the firepower used by infantry, vehicles, mechs, aerospace forces, and warships throughout the Core.
* **[Mechs and Military Technologies](../mechs/)** dominate planetary battlefields throughout known space.

Together, these systems bind hundreds of worlds into a single interconnected civilization.

## Lost Knowledge and Technological Stagnation

One of the defining characteristics of the modern era is the gradual loss of knowledge.

While humanity remains capable of producing advanced spacecraft, weapons, vehicles, and industrial equipment, certain technologies from the Golden Empire remain only partially understood. Some ancient systems continue to operate despite centuries of repairs and modifications, while others can no longer be reproduced at all.

This technological stagnation is particularly visible in areas such as capital ship construction, ancient industrial infrastructure, and certain branches of Hypercomplex Mathematics used in jump navigation.

As a result, many governments treat scientific archives, engineering records, and technical expertise as strategic assets every bit as valuable as military forces.

## Innovation and Competition

The Great Houses invest heavily in research programs, industrial expansion, military modernization, and scientific exploration. Competition between states and corporations drives continual improvements in weapons, electronics, aerospace systems, communications infrastructure, and manufacturing techniques.

The Orion Corporate is particularly renowned for technological innovation, while other powers often emphasize reliability, scale, craftsmanship, or military practicality depending upon their cultural priorities.

## Technology and Civilization

Most citizens rarely think about the immense technological systems that support their daily lives.

Every shipment of food, every interstellar message, every military deployment, and every voyage between the stars depends upon networks of engineers, scientists, navigators, manufacturers, technicians, and infrastructure operators working behind the scenes.

Without technology, the Core would fragment into isolated worlds almost immediately. It is not merely a collection of machines. It is the foundation that allows civilization itself to exist.
